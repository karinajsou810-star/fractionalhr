Case studies

Two engagements. Two different problems. Same approach.

Case study 01

When every consultant has their own system, nothing can scale

Professional servicesSMEFeb–Nov 2022

What was broken

Recruiting was happening — but every person doing it followed their own version of the process. No shared documentation. No standard for what a candidate profile should contain. No way to onboard a new consultant without losing weeks of handholding. The practice couldn't grow because the knowledge lived in people's heads, not in a system.

What I built

A three-phase recruiting model. A ten-category candidate profile template. A three-channel sourcing approach. A four-touchpoint post-placement follow-up sequence. Packaged as an internal training document and a client-facing version.

Outcome
New consultants could follow the full process from day one — no shadow period, no senior consultant required to walk them through it. Onboarding time for new recruiting staff dropped to zero ramp-up on process.
Case study 02

Seven years, zero coverage gaps: building a people function that runs without the founder

Private services3 staff + 7 vendors2018–2025

What was broken

Leadership worked off-site. The operation ran on three permanent staff and seven external vendors, with no documentation, no structured onboarding, and no visibility into scheduling, vendor performance, or budget from a distance. People decisions were made informally. Vendor relationships had no criteria — no way to evaluate performance, no threshold for when to end a contract. When something went wrong, it went to the founder. There was no system to prevent that.

What I built

A three-document onboarding kit covering shift schedules, role-specific operational profiles, and contractor coordination — enabling full operational readiness from day one for any new hire. An integrated tracker consolidating team scheduling, vendor coordination, and budget monitoring into a single source of truth accessible to off-site leadership in real time. Formal performance criteria for all seven external vendors, including defined termination thresholds, so vendor decisions were based on documented standards rather than judgment calls. A structured recruitment process — screening 15 candidates over the engagement period, selecting three recurring placements that became reliable annual leave coverage for each permanent team member.

Outcome
50% efficiency gain in workflow management. Zero coverage gaps across seven years. Leadership could see operational status in real time, remotely, without asking anyone. The people function ran without their involvement in day-to-day decisions.
